# AnalystLab Africa Internship: Data Cleaning and Exploratory Data Analysis
Overview

This project covers Week 1 and Week 2 of the AnalystLab Africa Data Analytics Internship Program. It applies a full data cleaning and exploratory data analysis workflow to two real-world datasets: an e-commerce transactions dataset and a Netflix content catalog. 
The focus was on building a reliable, well-documented cleaning process, then extracting meaningful insights through analysis and visualization.

## Datasets

1. Online Retail (E-commerce Transactions)
541,909 rows of UK-based wholesale retail transaction data, covering products, quantities, prices, customers, and countries.
Source: [Kaggle - Online Retail Dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/vijayuv/onlineretail)

2. Netflix Movies and TV Shows
8,807 rows covering Netflix's content catalog, including titles, genres, countries, ratings, and release information.
Source: [Kaggle - Netflix Shows Dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shivamb/netflix-shows)

## Tools Used

- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib)
- Jupyter Notebook (VS Code)
- Microsoft Word (summary reporting)

## Process

1. Dataset Understanding: Reviewed structure, data types, and identifiers for both datasets.
2. Data Cleaning: Identified and handled missing values, removed duplicates, corrected invalid entries, and standardized formats.
3. Exploratory Data Analysis: Generated summary statistics and performed five or more analyses per dataset.
4. Visualization: Built five charts per dataset covering distributions, trends, and comparisons.
5. Insights: Documented five key findings per dataset, each grounded in the analysis and charts produced.

## Key Findings

Online Retail
- The United Kingdom generated the vast majority of revenue at £8.19 million, far ahead of any other country.
- Monthly revenue grew steadily through 2011, peaking in November.
- A small number of customers accounted for a disproportionate share of total revenue.
- Most transactions were priced under £5, reflecting a catalog of low-cost goods.
- Negative quantity transactions were confirmed as legitimate order cancellations, not data errors.

Netflix
- Movies made up 69.7 percent of the catalog compared to 30.3 percent TV Shows.
- Content additions grew sharply between 2015 and 2019, peaking at over 2,000 titles added in 2019.
- The United States led content origin, followed by India, the United Kingdom, Japan, and South Korea.
- International Movies and Dramas were the two most common genres by a wide margin.
- TV-MA was the most common content rating, indicating a catalog weighted toward mature audiences.
